Large Language Models acing Chartered Accountancy

Jatin Gupta, Akhil Sharma, Saransh Singhania, Mohammad Adnan, Sakshi Deo, Dr. Ali Imam Abidi*, Keshav Gupta
MoStart2025: International Conference on Digital Transformation in Education and Artificial Intelligence Applications
[paper]

We present CA-Ben, a benchmark designed to evaluate the financial, legal, and quantitative reasoning capabilities of advanced language models in the Indian context. While Large Language Models (LLMs) are transforming financial practices through breakthroughs in Natural Language Processing (NLP), their ability to handle domain-specific financial knowledge remains unclear. CA-Ben addresses this gap by leveraging structured Q&A datasets drawn from the Chartered Accountancy exams conducted by the Institute of Chartered Accountants of India (ICAI), covering foundational to advanced levels. We evaluate six leading LLMs—GPT-4o, LLAMA 3.3 70B, LLAMA 3.1 405B, MISTRAL Large, Claude 3.5 Sonnet, and Microsoft Phi 4—using standardized protocols. Results show performance differences, with Claude 3.5 Sonnet and GPT-4o leading in conceptual and legal reasoning. However, numerical accuracy and legal interpretation remain key challenges. Our findings highlight both the promise and the current limits of LLMs, pointing to the need for hybrid reasoning approaches and retrieval-augmented generation for more robust financial AI systems.

Challenges with Current Health Information Systems

Akhil Sharma, Jatin Gupta, Prof. Shajee Mohan
CRC Press | In Book, Digital Health: Managing and Leveraging Health Information Systems
[chapter]

We present an in-depth exploration of Hospital Information Systems (HIS) and their pivotal role in transforming modern healthcare. This chapter examines critical challenges related to data management, security, user adoption, and regulatory compliance, emphasizing the need for effective communication among stakeholders and a robust IT infrastructure as foundational elements for successful HIS deployment. Using a case study from Isfahan province, the chapter identifies key success factors and barriers in implementing HIS, highlighting the importance of strategic planning and change management. Furthermore, it discusses how emerging technologies like artificial intelligence and blockchain can unlock new, value-driven pathways for patient care—enhancing not only service delivery but also the overall efficiency and safety of healthcare systems in a patient-centric environment.

Vaani: Transforming Healthcare and Enhancing Communication for the Deaf and Mute Community with Real-Time Translation

Jatin Gupta, Rajat Singhal, Navya Sharma, Brijesh Yadav, Vedant Tiwari
ICRTICC-2025: International Conference on Recent Trends in Intelligent Computing and Communication
[paper]

We present Vaani, a comprehensive communication solution designed to bridge the gap for individuals with hearing and speech impairments, particularly in critical settings like healthcare. Communication barriers in such contexts often hinder effective interaction, as individuals face difficulties due to language and speech limitations. Existing tools frequently lack real-time, multilingual support and struggle to meet the demands of diverse, often rural environments. Vaani addresses these challenges by integrating real-time speech-to-text (STT) and text-to-speech (TTS) capabilities, offering multilingual functionality along with offline support—essential for accessibility in remote regions. Additionally, the system ensures secure communication through encryption and decryption mechanisms. Results demonstrate Vaani’s effectiveness in enabling barrier-free dialogue, significantly enhancing communication experiences within healthcare and extending its impact to broader social interactions.

Indian Sign Language Detection for Real-Time Translation using Machine Learning

Rajat Singhal, Jatin Gupta, Akhil Sharma, Anushka Gupta, Navya Sharma
RAIT-2025: International Conference on Recent Advances in Information Technology
[paper]

We present a vision-based solution to enhance communication for the deaf and mute communities through Indian Sign Language (ISL) recognition. Gestural language, consisting of hand gestures and body movements, forms the foundation of communication in these communities, relying on visual and spatial patterns known as sign languages. However, individuals in the deaf community often face major challenges due to limited access to reliable interpretation tools. To address this, our work focuses on detecting ISL using Convolutional Neural Networks (CNN), achieving a high accuracy of 99.95% on the ISL dataset. The system leverages Mediapipe for efficient hand motion detection and is evaluated using accuracy, F1 score, and precision-recall metrics. This research outlines the complete pipeline—from preprocessing to model architecture and classification—offering a step forward in building inclusive communication technologies.

Legal Assist AI-Leveraging Transformer based Model for Effective Legal Assistance

Jatin Gupta, Akhil Sharma, Saransh Singhania, Dr. Ali Imam Abidi*
Under Review at Data and Knowledge Engineering
[preprint]

We present Legal Assist AI, a transformer-based model aimed at improving access to legal assistance in India. Many citizens struggle to access legal rights due to limited awareness and information. Legal Assist AI leverages advanced natural language processing (NLP) to respond to both subjective and objective legal queries, benefiting legal professionals, scholars, and the general public. Fine-tuned on datasets like Lawyer_GPT_India and the All India Bar Examination (AIBE) dataset, it demonstrates a robust understanding of Indian law. Evaluated against models like GPT-3.5 Turbo and Mistral 7B, it achieved a 60.08% score on the AIBE, excelling in legal reasoning and accuracy. Unlike other models, it avoids issues like hallucinations, ensuring reliability for real-world legal use. Future updates aim to expand its capabilities to support multilingual and case-specific queries.

[code]

CA-ThinkFlow is an AI-powered financial consulting application designed to assist users with various financial queries. Built using Streamlit and Langchain, this application leverages advanced language models to provide accurate and context-aware responses to user questions related to finance. The system implements Retrieval Augmented Generation (RAG) to enhance response accuracy by referencing a curated knowledge base of financial documents and regulations. [code]

The code establishes a robust framework for detecting tampered images using Error Level Analysis (ELA) and a Convolutional Neural Network (CNN). ELA highlights compression artifacts indicative of tampering, and images are preprocessed, normalized, and resized to a fixed dimension. The dataset, containing authentic and tampered images, is labeled and split into training, validation, and test sets. Data augmentation is applied to enhance generalization, and the model is trained with early stopping. Evaluation includes confusion matrices, accuracy, precision, and F1-scores, achieving ~90% accuracy. The pipeline also visualizes tampered regions and predicts authenticity with high confidence, demonstrating its efficacy for digital image forensics.
